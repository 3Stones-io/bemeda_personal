üîç What is USE?

Unified Scenario Engineering (USE) is a collaborative approach that transforms vague requirements into crystal-clear, executable scenarios using three key innovations:

1. Actor-Based Thinking

Instead of thinking in terms of "users" and "systems," we identify specific actors - the real people and systems that participate in your scenarios.

The Three Types of Actors

üë•
Human Actors

Real people with goals, emotions, and constraints

‚öôÔ∏è
System Actors

Technical components that process and respond

üñ•Ô∏è
Interface Actors

UX/UI elements that facilitate interactions

2. Step-Centric Component Organization

We organize work around Business Scenarios (S1-S19) supported by UX and Technical components:

üéØ
Business Scenarios (S1-S19)

Primary end-to-end workflows that deliver business value to actors

üé®
UX Components (U1-UN)

User interface elements that support individual business steps

‚öôÔ∏è
Technical Components (T1-TN)

Backend APIs and systems that enable business step functionality

3. Executable Specifications

Every scenario is written in a format that can be:

  • ‚úÖ Understood by business stakeholders
  • ‚úÖ Validated through automated testing
  • ‚úÖ Executed as part of your development process
  • ‚úÖ Tracked for progress and completion

üìã A Real USE Scenario

Here's how we'd write a scenario for our healthcare recruitment platform:

Scenario: Healthcare Organisation Receives Cold Call

Given a Healthcare Organisation exists as a qualified prospect
And the Sales Team has researched their staffing needs
When the Sales Team calls the Healthcare Organisation
And presents our value proposition
And explains our placement success rate of "92%"
And offers a "no placement, no fee" guarantee
Then the Healthcare Organisation should understand our value proposition
And their understanding level should be at least 8 out of 10
And they should express interest in our services
And the interaction should be logged in the CRM System

# Parallel components that support this step:
And the following components should execute successfully:
  | Component | Type | Expected Result |
  | U1 | UX | Dashboard shows call |
  | T1 | Technical | CRM logging successful |
  | U25 | UX | Form fields validated |

Notice what this scenario tells us:

  • üéØ Clear Success Criteria: "understanding level should be at least 8 out of 10"
  • üë• Specific Actors: Healthcare Organisation, Sales Team, CRM System
  • üîó Component Dependencies: U1 (dashboard), T1 (API), U25 (forms) support this step
  • ‚úÖ Measurable Outcomes: We can actually test if this works
